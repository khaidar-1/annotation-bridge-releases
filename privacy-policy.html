Annotation Bridge — Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 10, 2026

Overview

Annotation Bridge is a browser extension that captures text highlights and page metadata from web pages and sends them to the Annotation Bridge desktop application running on your own computer. No data is sent to any external server.

Data Collection

Annotation Bridge does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers. All captured data (highlighted text, page title, URL, author, and publication metadata) is sent exclusively to http://127.0.0.1 or http://localhost — your own machine.

  • No analytics or telemetry
  • No third-party tracking scripts
  • No cookies set by the extension
  • No remote servers contacted

Permissions Explained

  • activeTab — Read the current page's content when you make a selection. Only activated by user action.
  • storage — Persist your server port preference and offline annotation queue locally in the browser.
  • contextMenus — Add a "Save to Annotation Bridge" option to the right-click menu.
  • alarms (Chrome only) — Retry sending queued annotations when the desktop app was temporarily offline.
  • host_permissions (localhost) — Send captured annotations to the Annotation Bridge desktop app on your machine.

Data Storage

The extension stores two items in your browser's local storage:

  • Server port — The port number for the local desktop app (default: 24242).
  • Offline queue — Annotations captured while the desktop app is not running, automatically flushed when the app becomes available.

All stored data remains on your device and is never transmitted externally.

Third-Party Services

Annotation Bridge does not integrate with or send data to any third-party services.
